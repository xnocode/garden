The Zettelkasten is a note-taking method developed by the German sociologist Niklas Luhmann, who used it to write something like seventy books and four hundred articles over his career. The system is deceptively simple: write small, link densely, give every note an ID. Its power comes from what those constraints do to your thinking.

## Three principles

**Atomicity.** One idea per note. Not one topic — one *idea*. If a note tries to do two things, split it. The reason is that atomic notes can be recombined infinitely; bloated notes can only be read in the order you wrote them. An atomic note titled "the stream vs. the garden" can join a future essay you haven't imagined yet. A six-paragraph note titled "stuff about gardens" cannot. ^atomicity

**Unique IDs.** Every note gets a stable identifier. Luhmann used hierarchical numbers (`21/3d7a5`); in a digital garden the filename or title does the job. The point is permanence: a link from 2019 should still resolve in 2030. This is why I'm cautious about renaming notes — see [[Wikilinks and Backlinks]].

**Linking.** Notes connect to other notes explicitly. Links are how a Zettelkasten becomes more than a pile. When you add a note, you ask: what does this disagree with, extend, exemplify? Over time, clusters form on their own.

> [!tip] The note as a conversation
> A good Zettelkasten note doesn't just record a fact — it takes a position. Write it as if you're explaining it to a future self who will push back. That friction is what makes the note useful later.

## What it isn't

The Zettelkasten is not a filing system. You don't sort notes into folders by topic; you let links do the organizing. It's also not a journal — notes should be self-contained, not tied to the day you wrote them. And it's not a quote collection; capturing a quote without your own gloss is usually a waste, because you won't remember why you saved it.

## My checklist for a new note

- [x] Give it a precise, claim-shaped title
- [x] Write the idea in my own words
- [ ] Link it to at least two existing notes
- [ ] Add a source if it came from somewhere
- [ ] Re-read it in a week and revise

The Zettelkasten is the discipline layer that makes a garden like [[Digital Gardens]] navigable. Pair it with [[Building a Second Brain]] for the upstream capture habit, and with [[The Art of Note Taking]] for the softer philosophy of why any of this is worth doing.
