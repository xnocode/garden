It felt so real that even after waking up, the dream stayed in my mind like a memory instead of imagination.

I was traveling through my own city with only 230 Tk left in my pocket. The streets looked familiar, yet somehow different quieter, heavier, like the city was hiding something. Evening light slowly faded into orange shadows when I met her.

A girl with a dark screen on her phone stood near the roadside, looking lost. I couldn’t clearly remember her face after waking up, but in the dream she looked beautiful to me in a strange, calm way. She asked for help because she wanted to return home. Her home was in Meherpur.

The strange thing was I barely even knew where Meherpur was. Maybe I had heard the name before, somewhere in passing. In the dream, I searched it on Google and discovered it was somewhere beyond Rajshahi. Somehow that tiny detail felt important.

She wanted to return by train.

We started talking while searching for the station and then took a rickshaw together. What surprised me most in the dream was how natural everything felt. Sitting beside her didn’t feel awkward at all. There was no pressure, no overthinking just comfort, like we had known each other for a long time without actually knowing each other.

Later I realized one of my friends somehow knew her too, and strangely, I knew that friend in the dream world as well. The two of us reached the station together, but by then evening had already turned dark. We missed the train.

The next train was hours away.

So we wandered around the nearby area while waiting. The streets were crowded, and at one point we walked into a rally — some kind of procession moving loudly through the road. People were shouting slogans, lights were flashing, and the crowd kept pushing from every direction.

Instinctively, I placed my hand on her shoulder so we wouldn’t get separated. Then suddenly I realized she was a girl, and without thinking too much, I moved my hand away and held her hand instead.

What felt strange was that neither of us seemed uncomfortable. There was silence between us, but it wasn’t empty silence. It felt safe.

After leaving the crowd, we found an old house near the station where we went to ask for information about the train. But something about the people inside felt wrong immediately. Their eyes, their voices everything carried bad intentions. The air suddenly became heavy.

And then we ran.

We ran through dark roads, past unfamiliar corners, hearing footsteps behind us even if nobody was there. The city no longer felt like my city anymore.

Right before anything else could happen I woke up.

> “I don’t want to marry anyone. But if it hadn’t been a dream, I think I would’ve asked her if she would marry me.”
