Welcome in. If you're wondering how to actually move through this place, here's a short orientation. A digital garden rewards wandering more than reading top-to-bottom, so the tools below are really just suggestions for getting delightfully lost.

## Search

Hit **⌘K** (or **Ctrl+K** on Windows/Linux) anywhere to open the command palette. Type a few letters and you'll see matching notes — titles, aliases, and tags all surface here. It's the fastest way to jump to a thought you half-remember.

## The graph

Every note here is connected to others through [[Wikilinks and Backlinks]]. The graph view renders those connections as a small constellation of nodes. It's not just decoration — it's a map of how ideas depend on each other. Dense clusters usually mean I've been thinking hard about something.

## Backlinks

At the bottom of every note you'll find a **backlinks** section: every other note that links *to* the one you're reading. Backlinks are the soul of a garden. They turn a one-way citation into a conversation. See [[Digital Gardens]] for why this matters.

## Tags and the explorer

Notes carry tags like `#essay` or `#reference/organization` — browse them in the tag index, or use the file explorer in the sidebar to skim everything at once. [[Tags and Organization]] goes deeper on how I nest and use them.

## A note on drafts

Not every note is published. Some are still cooking privately. If you can see it, it's meant to be read — though "meant to be read" and "finished" are different things. The full reference for formatting magic lives at [[Callouts Reference]].

Go wander.
